Turn games or positions

Read a chess book is a great pleasure, but those huge amounts of chessboards with countless positions hold back the progress, you know what I mean? ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)

With Diagram you can transcribe that position in a somewhat playable in 1 minute! You don't need to know what is the FEN code or the PGN notation, although you should already know them.

Here you create the board from a position (when you don't know what were the moves in the evolution of the game, for example, studying tactics) which would be a FEN code

or

you can enter the moves of a game, studying openings or strategy, for example, or to rebuild the entire game to analyze a beautiful checkmate, which would be a PGN notation.

into readable QR-Codes

And this position that you just created will turn into a beautiful QR-Code pressing a simple, single and green button.

You don't need to understand anything about QR-Codes, only that they keep the data necessary for the Diagram app recognize and transform this information into a game within app. And believe me, it happens in 2 seconds!

All information is included in this QR-Code, such as, the position of the pieces, who is the next side to move, castling rights and even an en-passant square.

I'm sure you were already thinking about the possibility, and the answer is YES, you can create positions that are impossible to be played, for example, put two Kings of the same color, or say that there is right to castle without having any rook left, and this may result in errors when scanning this code.

then scan and play!

Et voilà! The game that was in your book now is in your Diagram app. You can browse through the moves or play the position against Stockfish IA.
